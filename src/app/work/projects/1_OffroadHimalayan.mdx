## Overview

As a solo project, I developed a custom car controller in Unity for an open-world driving simulator, tackling the inherent challenges of Wheel Colliders by fine-tuning suspension, friction, and handling dynamics from the ground up. I discarded Unity's default settings and implemented my own friction and torque configurations to achieve realistic vehicle behavior. To further elevate the simulator experience, I also built a hardware-based controller, adding a tactile and immersive layer to the driving feel. Completing this project independently deepened my passion for vehicle mechanics and physics, and brought me immense satisfaction in seeing the full vision realized through my own efforts.

## Key Features

- **Vehicle Physics**: Custom friction and torque system replacing Unity’s default Wheel Collider settings for realistic handling and suspension behavior.
- **Character Controller**:  Implemented a free-roaming on-foot system that enables players to collect resources like wood, physically load collected items onto a truck, and transport them to the store for sale. 
- **Local Store**: Implemented a persistent local store where players can purchase vehicles and apply upgrades, with a fully functional currency system.
- **Console level graphics** Achieved high-end graphical standards and immersive gameplay on mobile devices, making the environment feel rich and responsive.
- **Game Optimization**: Optimized the game to run smoothly on mid-tier Android devices while maintaining console-level graphics by reducing batch count, optimizing materials, implementing occlusion culling, and managing asset packs efficiently.
- **Settings**: Developed a comprehensive settings menu allowing players to adjust graphics quality and control preferences.
- **Ads and IAP **: Integrated advertisements, resolved revenue leakage issues, and implemented in-app purchases (IAP) for monetization.

## Challenges and Learnings
- **Optimizing for Low to High-End Phones**: One of the biggest challenges was ensuring consistent performance across a wide range of mobile devices. I implemented dynamic quality settings that allow the game to scale graphics, draw distance, and physics accuracy based on the device's capabilities. This involved extensive testing on multiple hardware tiers and fine-tuning rendering parameters to maintain a smooth framerate without compromising the visual experience on high-end devices.
- **Gameplay Feel**: Balancing realistic vehicle physics with enjoyable, responsive handling was a deeply iterative process. I spent countless hours fine-tuning torque curves, suspension stiffness, tire friction profiles, and weight transfer dynamics. Beyond raw physics, I focused on subtle details like camera motion, road feedback through the hardware controller, and audio cues to create that "perfect feel" where driving becomes intuitive and satisfying. This challenge ultimately became the most rewarding part of the project, as it required merging technical precision with player psychology.
